Photography in black & white

2019-10-05 By Anna Winther


Photography in black and white or grayscale are a popular expression for photo art. Its subtle expression make you pay more attention to the details and the feeling of the picture are a bit more calming and soothing than photos in color. To get the asolutely best photograph using grayscale Photoshop or Lightroom is a must. Where you can alter contrast, curves and other attributes to really make the photograph stand out.

Black and white photos can be a bit tricky to print since most printers use all their colors to print black and white. They actually mix colors together to get different shades of grey and black. For a photograpgher or someone who is really interested in art this can be a real hassle. You often see that the photo tends to have a slightly red, purple, green or blue shade. To get a real black and white photo your therefor need to use a printer that only uses the Black color, whether its ink or laser your print with.

Most photographers use large format printers, even though these are professional printers they rarely let you print with only black and grey inks. One slution is to get a RIP, a special software that forces the printer to only use choosen colors. This is the absolutely best way to get true black and white photos. Even though the software can be a bit expensive, your only pay for it once and will save a lot of money by not needing to adjust the colors all the time to get the perfetct photo.

Nature photos in winter

2019-10-01 By Anna Winther

Winter can be a tricky time to take photos since the light isn't that good and you often need to get up early to catch the best light. Another problem can be that nature is mostly greyish all the flowers are gone, the trees have lost their leaves and the gras isn't green anymore. One solution can be to photographg other parts of nature, that you don't really notice during summer, or parts of nature that really comes alive during winter.

Some of those areas is everything surrounding water. In winter, a frozen lake or a snowy mountain can be beautiful to photograph, even though they might not look that appealing during summer. I really recommend you to visit nature in different seasong so that you get a feel of which motives works best during which season, since they are rarely the same ones.

It's also a good idea to check the weather report closely during winter since most landscapes are much prettier when it has been snowing. Untouched snow is one of the most beautiful motives you can find accorindg to me! Another important part of taking photos during winter is to dress for the part. It can be challeging to be outside for several hours when it's cold and windy. And we all know that capturing that perfect photo can take time. So dress wisely and pay attention to the weather at your location.

I really hope you got some good tips from this post, and hopefully it will help you come closer to those beautiful winter photos.

Use Photoshop to edit your photos

2019-09-27 By Anna Winther


One of the absolutely best tools when it comes to editing is Photoshop. With Photoshop you can do pretty much everything and in enhances your photos big time! For everyont out there that loves eiditing programs as much as I do, I also recommend taking a closer look at Lightroom. It's a great program, and the possibility to edit photos on the go in your phone are so usefull. For those of you that hasn't spent so much time woring with different kind of eiditing software I recommend that you start with Lightroom first, since it's a bit easier for a new beginner.

When you work with black and white photos editing programs are so usefull and quite necessary. To be able to adjust contrast, hue, curves and levels makes the image pop. A great way to learn these programs is just to play around with them. Import your favorite photo and start to try out different tools.

There are a bunch of courses online where you can find good information if you want to learn Photoshop or Lightroom. Thats perfect for those of us who don't want to pay large amounts of money for paid courses. Hopefully you find as much use of editing software as I do, and if you have any questions, feel free to contact me. I'm happy to help!
